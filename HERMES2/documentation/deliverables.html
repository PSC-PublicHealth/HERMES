Explaining Deliverables and Shippables

In recent repository versions (around SVN r1109), we have changed the DemandModel functionality so that there are two separate abstract base classes of deliverables. These classes are:

  • Shippables: an object shipped across the supply chain, which may not individually be given to an end-user. Shippables may be used to prepare a Deliverable, and may be used in conjunction with other Deliverables. Their delivery is implied in the supply chain. An example of a Shippable might be diluent needed to reconstitute lyophilized vaccine, or in the case of fast food, the oil needed to fry fast food.
  • Deliverables: this is an object shipped across the supply chain, which must be prepared at delivery time before it is consequently given to a customer. As Joel explained it, "it is a something that a patient gets, and may require other Shippables before the patient can get it." The delivery of a deliverable is returned by their respective demand model. An example of a Deliverable might be the vaccine which is administered to the population, or the french fries given to a fast food restaurant patron.

There is a preparation process for deliverables which happens instantaneously. More data about these preparation + delivery processes can be found in the developer documentation here.

Auxillary Notes

  • DemandModels return collections of Deliverables only; the need for consumable Shippables are implied. Therefore, DemandModels display Deliverables only.
  • Fridges are Deliverables because they are Shippables which are not consumed to prepare any other Shippable for delivery.
